Fred Weasley was a very clever young man. He was calculating, perceptive, enterprising, and above all, highly ambitious with a flair for mischief. However, most of these wonderful qualities were entirely overlooked by those who knew him, and he was quite often simply referred to as a Troublemaker.

From the time he and his twin brother, George, were the tender age of two, they could topple any semblance of order in their home. They ambled about leaving chaos in their wake, much to the chagrin of their poor, desperate mother as she chased after them while shouting and pulling out her rapidly greying hair.

At the age of three, Fred and George became the eye of the proverbial storm, unfolding upon the world around them. First within their own back garden, and then as they grew older still, into the surrounding landscape where they spent many hours running rampant through Ottery St. Catchpole. Fred and George discovered lots of good hiding places and collected many specimens for their on-going experiments, honing their skills before they went off to Hogwarts and became the stuff of legends.

With all their mischievousness and determination, was it any wonder that they should become proprietors at eighteen, of one of the wizarding world’s most promising joke shops? Everyone seemed to agree that they were good lads with the right idea, and even their mother had to concede their success,eventually.

Weasleys’ Wizard Weezes.

For anyone not immediately taken in by the shameless alliteration, the shop front certainly turned their heads. The displays were bright and showy, as flashy as freshly minted galleons, and in this way secured the twins’ reputation practically overnight. Both young and old would gather in droves to marvel at the wonderous contrivances and eagerly purchase a multitude of products that guaranteed their satisfaction. They were never disappointed.

Laughter reined chiefly in their wake, filling them up completely and following them all the way home. Something to shield them against the tempestuous shift between good and evil, and the rise of dark power once more.

Such uncertainty.

Such fear and hopelessness.

And Messrs Weasley, knowing full well that they could be targeted for encouraging such optimism, spared themselves very little time worrying about it. It was all just for laughs. They never considered themselves courageous, never a shining beacon to weather the storm. But in the end, that’s just what they were. And it showed. Their mirth and their resourcefulness kept the wizarding world steadfast, even as they were forced to close the shop and go into hiding. They would not be deterred however and continued to use their voices to bolster the people and carry them through until the Final Battle at Hogwarts.

And it all started with Canary Creams and Fever Fudge. Clever, clever boys.

So, when the opportunity presented itself, Fred Weasley did not hesitate in acquiring his own pub. A sleepy little place tucked away from, but within sight of Everything. Oh, how he loved it! His pride was visible in every detail, even the most menial of tasks benefiting from his constant attention. And rather than feeling exhausted by the weight of it all, Fred was emboldened, keeping his establishment forever in tip-top order. There were floors to scrub, tabletops to wax, bar stools to bolster, lamps to light, glasses to fill, nibbles to garnish, and customers to chat with endlessly about absolutely nothing.

And while they waffled on as he hummed and nodded occasionally, Fred was able to indulge in his favourite pastime of all, polishing mugs. With a delicate microfiber cloth and a little elbow grease- such a silly muggle term- even the most roguish glassware would shine like Waterford by the time he was finished with it. The act of polishing each mug so carefully in front of said patrons was decidedly not for the benefit of any convenient eavesdropping.

The best part though, as far as Fred was concerned, the very best part , was being stationed at the bar passing a freshly polished pint or tumbler across the glistening walnut countertop. The seamless glide of low friction, glass whispering against wood. Flickered candlelight caught amidst deep facets, starbursts dancing all around. It was simply lovely to behold, and all thanks to the countless hours spent pampering that chocolate and honey coloured surface with Manchurian Tung Oil. Yes, Fred Weasley loved his pub very much.

He needn’t do everything by hand, of course, which he didn’t unless the mood took him. After all, Fred was once a fully qualified wizard with nearly two O.W.L.’s to his name, capable of charming away the chores whenever he pleased. But the satisfaction he got out of doing it the muggle way was unbeatable. Even his forays into lock picking and other non-magical enticements couldn’t contend with this.

Fred finally understood what his mother had meant about it building character, and all those times the persnickety Filch had wheezed and cackled at him during detentions. If he were still among the living, he would thank them for their constant tirades and apologize for their near fibrillation at his expense. Alas.

Yes, Fred Weasley never truly appreciated manual labor until after the day he died. It had made so little sense beforehand. Why would he want to waste his breath and his livelihood dusting the mantle? There were toilets to be blown up and prefect’s badges to steal. Inventing and still more inventing. Oh, the practical jokes! He missed it. He missed them . He missed George most of all. But Fred had his pub and his patrons whom he was proud to call friends, and if things weren’t quite the same as Diagon Alley or The Burrow, well… it was close enough.

Moths to a flame.

Sirius just stared at the man, his mouth parted slightly, and his brows knitted in bewilderment. “Don’t you want them to be happy? Don’t they deserve it while they’re still alive and well?” He asked desperately.

"……"

"……"

Severus examined the amber liquid in his glass intently and took a generous, silent sip.

Sirius slammed his fist down on the countertop. “Oh, come on, Snape! Part of you must be just the littlest bit of a hopeless romantic. Why not let go and feel happy for someone else for a change?”

Severus began reciting the twelve uses for dragon’s blood in his head. It was the only thing to keep him from grinding his teeth into dust as Black weathered his remaining fortitude. He was truly sick of speculating on an entirely theoretical relationship between two people that could not be more dissimilar to each other. What could he possibly do to stop this confounded fool? Agree with him? Never!

Somehow, Severus found himself acquiescing to the mad notion before he could stop it. “If you promise not to be so overbearing with this obsession of yours then I will concur that yes, maybe, there is a chance for them to be happy together, someday”. Another silent sip.

“I knew it! I told you love conquers all!’ Sirius picked up his glass and had it halfway to his lips before-

“What did I just say, Black?” Severus warned. A dark glint in his eyes.

“Alright.” Sirius snorted.

The mongrel sat there as smug as anything, with that self-satisfied grin on his pretentious face. Finally, Severus would get some peace and hopefully never hear another word on the subject again.

Then, he realized that if the mutt wasn’t talking about Draco and Harry, he’d just find something else to pester him with until his mind completely melted from the stupidity of it all. And it would most likely be talk of how miserably lonely he was without his Chew-Toy hanging off his every word and action. Better to nip that one in the bud before it could blossom.

“And you shouldn’t worry about being alone, either.” Severus remarked in what he hoped was a casual manner. “It’s really not all that bad.”

Sirius couldn’t help the way his mouth was hanging open like a goldfish, his glass still forgotten midway to his lips. “How can you say that!? How can you sit there and tell me that being lonely is a good thing?!”

“Easily.” Severus replied, pressing the matter. He then turned toward the wizened headmaster for reinforcement of this argument. “Albus, you don’t mind being alone, do you?”

The old man chuckled. “Not anymore, but I’ve had a long time to get used to the idea.”

“There, you see. Simple as that, Black.” Severus smirked.

“Of course,” Albus continued, “I was young once, too, and for a time, not alone. It was rather a difficult turning point in my life, but I would not have traded the feeling of being loved, however misunderstood it was at the time, for anything in the world.”

Severus pursed his lips then drained his remaining whiskey in one gulp.

Sirius chuckled and threw back his own drink. “Well, then.”

“Bugger off, Black.”

“Ha!” Sirius continued to chuckle quietly beside his brooding enemy, savouring this moment and filing it away for later. Then something occurred to him that he’d never given any thought to before. Pensively, he added, “You’re all alone, all the time. That must be difficult.” He could see Snape tensing out of the corner of his eye. “I mean, I’m only ever alone part of the time, and I can’t stand it. Absolutely hate it, in fact.”

“Don’t you dare to pity me, Black!” Severus snarled. He would not allow this fool to probe the matter any further.

“Alright! Don’t get your knickers in a twist!” Sirius put his palms up before the irate Bat next to him. “It was merely an observation.” Snape had reacted viciously to that notion, and Sirius couldn’t help but wonder if the man was just as bereft as he was at times. That was rather… gloomy.

However, Sirius did not pity Snape, no, never pity. It felt more like comradery, a kinship, even. They were both facing a similar situation, what with the whole being lonely business, and they both had oodles of time on their hands. “I suppose you and I aren’t really all that different. Both knocking about in the same, shapeless place forever more, being alone and miserable. We’re rather a pair, aren’t we?”

Severus curled his lip in disdain. “How utterly disturbing.”

Sirius chuckled again, but without any of his usual bite. He was still caught up in that rather interesting thought. A pair. Hmmm… disturbing. Very disturbing. Wasn’t it? But what if- No. No, surely not…

And yet…

Sirius carefully glanced to his left again, studying his childhood nemesis from the corner of his eye more intently than he’d ever done before. He watched as Snape took another slow and deliberate sip, his firm, yet supple lip lingering at the rim of the glass momentarily before pulling away. When did that happen? Weren’t his lips always thin and menacing? Oh, right! Must have been all that sneering distorting them so.

Snape spent what felt like an eternity savoring the liquid in his mouth, then suddenly swallowed. The unmarred column of his pale neck showing just enough above his high collar that Sirius could see the muscles shift and the bob of his Adam’s Apple. Such a mundane thing and yet… The effect lent itself to other possibilities in his mind. Oh, Merlin.

Ok, Sirius realized, he’d let himself get carried away. That was an easy thing to do when you were bored, and possibly a little desperate. But there was nothing, nothing that should have prompted that sort of astute observation of Snape. It was wrong on so many levels, and not something he would ever entertain for any reason. Ever. In fact, Sirius told himself, as he would never contemplate such a thing, the thought was actually quite harmless. It wasn’t as if it would ever happen again.

Pale… Supple… Strong. Lithe. Neck, lips, hands, body. Manipulating- heaving- writhing against- Ahh! No!... No, no, no, no, not that! Never that! Never. Never. Never. His imagination had gone far enough for one eternity. Sirius had to put those entirely unwelcome thoughts from his mind immediately and permanently. They had no business being there. None at all. Their persistence would only pollute his sanity until there was nothing left! Sirius could not allow that. Pull yourself together, man!

But…

But…

With great effort, he forced himself to breathe deeply and approach this matter clinically. That was the best way to deal with a problem of this magnitude. Right, so, what exactly was he looking at? A man, yes, but what sort? There wasn’t anything truly remarkable about him, surely, otherwise Sirius would have discovered that ages ago.

So, what were the glaringly conspicuous things that made him so ordinary, then? His nose for a start. It was his most prominent feature. There wasn’t a person in existence who would forget that whopper upon his face. Sirius studied the contours of Snape’s nose intently.

The sharp hook that had, in his youth, made Snape look more like a bird of prey than a boy was no longer quite so impressive. His high cheekbones had managed to balance out his features rather well since then, giving the man a distinguished appearance. Aquiline and chiseled. Unwavering. Solid. Just like the man himself. Why hadn’t Sirius ever noticed that before? What else have I been missing all this time?

Well…. Moving on. Snape’s hair. It was the butt of many jokes and played heavily in the roll of some of the Marauder’s pranks. How many times had Sirius referred to him as the Greasy Git when they were at school? It was practically slimy back then. But now… now, it appeared almost silky in the lamp light. But that couldn’t be right, could it? Sirius wasn’t sure, but it didn’t look greasy or grimy from where he was sitting. He barely resisted the urge to run his fingers through it and test his theory.

Alright. So those physical barriers were dismantled, but there were plenty of other things wrong with Snape, he was sure of it. Sirius looked the man up and down. He was lanky… No, that wasn’t right either. Perhaps he had been at school, but not anymore. Even with his billowing robes hindering a proper assessment, Sirius could tell there was an underlying confidence to Snape, much like a runner’s build. Probably acquired that during those long years of playing double agent.

Hmm… he smelled… bad? Nope. Once again, his previous odor was probably due to his involvement with various potion ingredients and hovering over a lit cauldron all day long.

Ah, his teeth! …. Sirius sighed. He’d never really cared about that one at all. In fact, if he were truly honest with himself, appearances made very little difference in the long run. Although, that might have been because he was just a tab bit self-absorbed, and he knew he was radiant enough for an entire room full of people. But with Snape, well, it had to make a difference. It helped to keep their hatred for each other going. Which was important. Very important. Wait, why was it important?

Sirius didn’t want to delve too deeply into that one. He could feel the weight of it pushing against the barrier of his mind even now, ready to break forth if he so much as tapped at the keystone. He had to stop this train of thought before he allowed himself to go completely mad from it.

So, what? He no longer had the excuse of a physical repulsion to the man. That made no difference. He still hated that self-assured, forceful stride, and the passionate sneers and snark and constant insults, and the way his inviting lips curled when he was excited, and when he tantalizingly raised his eyebrow before he was about to berate someone, and that velvety hum of his voice as he preyed upon a weak point in the conversation, and those fathomless pools of onyx that held a secret no person could ever hope to decipher with his gift for Occlumency, and Legilimency, and Dueling, with those hands, those bloody dexterous hands that could-. Oh, Fuck, fuck, fuckity-FUCK!

It was truly maddening. He, Sirius Black was mad. Utterly, completely, stupidly mad for having such thoughts. Yes, that was the truth of it, he’d gone completely round the twist contemplating such dangerous things that should never have been contemplated in the first place. Dangerous… Very dangerous…

Sirius sat quietly for a long moment with a rather odd look on his face, staring at the wall before him. He tilted his head to the side, furrowing his brows in concentration. In for a Knut…

None of this went unnoticed by Fred, who was still polishing the same mug he’d started on a half hour ago. Sirius pushed his empty tumbler toward him automatically, his eyes focused on something unseen, and Fred begrudgingly passed him a new one.

As he returned to his half-hearted task of polishing, Fred studied the proud Gryffindor before him. He could practically see the gears turning inside Sirius’ head as a flash of something mad stole across his face, and the familiar twinge of magic filled the air. Fred was standing close enough that he could feel the erratic prickling all over his skin and hastily wondered if the others sensed it as well.

Something was about to happen, though Fred was unsure what the outcome might be. Sirius was often spontaneous and although they could usually predict his turns, they still had trouble keeping pace with him. What was going on inside that head of his to cause such a strong reaction? He hoped it wouldn’t be anything explosive, having to rebuild his pub from little bits and pieces would be a nightmare. Whatever it was, Fred was sure they’d all find out soon enough.

Suddenly, the tension that was building all around them dissolved and the distant look in Sirius’ eyes faded away to reveal their familiar gleaming intensity. He was entirely too calm as he brought his tumbler to his lips, draining it in one swallow.

“Snape?” Sirius ventured quietly, keeping his eyes trained on the empty glass in his own hand.

“Yes?” Severus replied equally quiet, only a hint of disdain clipping the word. He didn’t like Black’s tone. There was something troubling in its wake, something he’d not care to examine nor wish to facilitate in its passing as it stretched out before him.

“Can I buy you a drink?” Sirius asked timidly.

Severus nearly dropped his Glencairn. He turned ever so slowly towards Black, his eyes narrowing to mere slits. “Why?” he asked, his voice gravely, barely above a whisper.

“Well, that’s what friends do, don’t they?” Sirius stated as if this were obvious even for a toadstool.

If the room wasn’t quiet before, it certainly was now. Fred abandoned the pretense of polishing his mug all together.

Friends? Severus was speechless. Again. It was becoming far too commonplace for his liking, and he swore to himself that it would end here and now. His barbed tongue was poised to deliver a scathing rebuttal, but his mind forced himself to dissect the meaning behind the word that Black had so carelessly thrown at him.

Friends? What in all of Hades had convinced the man that they were anything other than heated rivals? Not one day passed without them trying to goad each other into paroxysm or hex each other out of existence.

Friends? They utterly loathed one another.

Friends? How could the man be so idiotic as to even suggest that such a thing was possible between them?

Friends? No one would ever believe it.

These thoughts commandeered all his leverage and turned his insults to sludge in his mouth. When Severus finally found his voice again, he said the only plausible thing that came to mind. “We are not friends, Black.”

“Well,” Sirius focused on an errant piece of lint down the front of his shirt, “maybe we could be. I mean, after all, we’re just floating around here with nothing better to do. We should commiserate together.” He flicked the lint away without looking up. He hoped his words would come off as sincere, but seeing as this was a whole new experience for him with regards to Snape, he highly doubted it.

What was Black playing at? He would never be so humble nor so genuine as to offer Severus such an enterprise. There must be a catch somewhere, but the tangled threads were weaved together in such a relentless fashion that Severus could not begin to pick them apart. Well, he’d played along thus far, hadn’t he? He would simply need to bide his time before striking back against his tormentor. “A drink?”

“Yes.”

“You… want… to buy me… a drink?”

“Yes.”

“And you think this is an appropriate action to take… because you believe we are friends?”

“Yes.”

“Why would you assume that we are friends?”

“I’ve already told you. We have the same issue for once- as ghastly as that sounds- and we need a little mutual support, don’t you think?”

“……”

“We’re in the same boat… Like a pair of old shoes? Birds of a feather… um, swimming upstream, or, well… Ah! Two peas in a pod! That’s it! Yeh, we’re um… like two peas… in a pod.”

“Peas?”

“Yes.”

“Peas?”

“YES! You idiot! And I think we deserve a drink together!” Sirius shouldn’t have shouted, but the man was making this so bloody difficult and all over a harmless little drink.

“Just a drink, then.” Severus’s glared at him accusingly, his tone finite. He applauded his own efforts at resisting to hex the mutt.

Sirius looked at him incredulously. “Of course! I wasn’t implying anything else.” Was he? Possibly- No! Well, maybe? Is that how it sounded to Snape? He’d only meant… but if the man thought… and if somehow things did… Was that wrong? Shit. Sirius was truly bollocking this whole thing up. And now he was blushing like a bloody third year in front of him as well. Wonderful.

“I should hope not.” Severus snipped suggestively. The words were out of his mouth before he could stop them, the honeyed undercurrent utterly foreign to his usual disposition. What the hell am I doing? His response made it sound as if he were actively contemplating Black’s bizarre euphemism! It would never happen, no matter how desperate he was for companionship. His mental faculties could be in tatters and Severus would still not yield to something so unpalatable.

Black was a violent, petulant, imbecilic nuisance that he wanted nothing to do with whatsoever.

Severus forced himself to slow his breathing and analyse the situation. What was Black really insinuating? Surely, it couldn’t be anything as absurd as… Severus was doing his level best to ignore the many thoughts that seemed to plague him all at once. He would not allow himself to entertain such a notion, he simply wouldn’t.

He rationalised that this must all be part of the insidious game that Black was playing, to which he should continue his own part to uncover the plot. That helped to ground him. Yes, Severus was feeling more in control by the second, his focus returning tenfold, and he vowed to see this despicable charade come to an end.

He was a spy after all. So many years of careful observation gave his mind a granite edge and now he would harness that ability to dismantle Black. Stealthily he peered through the curtain of raven hair surrounding his face and observed.

Black was off with the fairies, staring at that ridiculous Board on the wall before him. Perfect. Severus allowed himself to continue cataloging his thoughts from earlier when their hands had met briefly. That same hand was fitted loosely around his empty glass, his forefinger occasionally running a languid circle around the rim. The movement bared his wrist with each turn, allowing a small runic tattoo to peek out from his well-tailored leather jacket before disappearing again up his sleeve.

Severus wondered what it would be like to brush his thumb across the dark ink, to trace its outline, to press ever so slightly against it until he could feel the man’s pulse hammering beneath. Now that was a dangerous thought. It led to more dangerous thoughts, things he could never admit to himself before.

Severus was not blind, nor was he stupid. No one could deny the allure that Black possessed. His immaculate, luminous hair. His radiant gaze. That sinuously lithe build and firm backside. Those were merely the tip of the iceberg for the unfathomable list of Black’s physical traits. And he was not advanced by his looks alone, no, despite Severus’ insistence that Black was a complete idiot, he was clever enough to be a top student year after year. And that charming, charismatic aura of his was… overwhelming, to say the least.

Black’s boundless energy that seeped into everything around him, consuming and roiling with its infectious fervor, was most probably the worst of it. Severus could feel the suffocating constraints of it all around him whenever Black was nearby. He’d tread against it from the time he was eleven years old, barely keeping his head above water, never knowing when he’d finally succumb from sheer exhaustion.

Black was an enigma, a loathsome and despicable one that Severus should never have put such consideration into, but like everyone else he was swept away by the man’s prowess. Oh, it was infuriating that a person could be so, so… fascinating to anyone! Not unlike the other one you cannot admit to having noticed these past ten years.

The silence around them was palpable but neither man seemed to notice. Fred gave them each a refill and quietly began polishing another glass. Both men sat in silence as they absorbed their drinks.

After several minutes of deathly quiet from the room, Sirius finally cleared his throat and asked, “But if I was, what exactly would I have to do to warrant such a thing?”

Even in death Severus was surprised by the deranged man’s gall. Did he truly mean exploits beyond that of a simple drink? It was preposterous! “You mean, a date?” he asked perplexedly, disbelief clearly etched in his features.

“Maybe.” Sirius was still looking at the front of his shirt, searching for another piece of nonexistent lint to brush away. What the fuck am I doing?!

What the fuck is he doing? Severus carefully scrutinised Black, looking for the trap he knew was waiting for him. It must be there somewhere, but as he continued to observe, he saw only the man, a rather anxious looking one at that. The fidgeting, the fixation over his clothes, the gentle tremor of his fingers.

Severus could barely contain the gasp threatening to escape him. Black was serious. Oh, I hate that stupid joke!

Black wanted… more? Impossible! This was mad, all of it! But he’d sworn to see this through. What if there was no scheme? What if something else did manifest between them? Severus’ curiosity was threatening to overwhelm him entirely.

He decided on a new tactic, one that hopefully would oust the mutt if he were up to no good. And if he was, Severus would flay him for it. If he wasn’t…, could he go through the motions with Black and his farse of a date? Would it go further still? In that moment, Severus no longer cared, the intrigue was too great for him. Let the true games begin. “One drink does not constitute a date.” He quipped before polishing off his scotch.

Sirius’s head shot up to look at the Board in front of him. “Alright then, just for curiosities sake, what would be your idea of a date?” He hasn’t hexed me yet.

Severus felt almost giddy as he watched Black squirm in his seat. He liked that, and wondered what else might make him squirm if…

Like the snake he was, Severus slowly slithered towards to his prey, closing what distance remained between them, and whispered ideas into his ear.

Sirius’ eyes widened almost imperceptibly, his pupils dilating from the deep, velvety tone supplying such wicked thoughts to his mind. He could not suppress the shutter that overcame him. This is madness.

Fred’s face was redder than his hair, having stood just close enough to somehow overhear the last few secret words. Some things should not be known about one’s professor. Ever. He quickly turned to finish polishing the glass elsewhere.

Silence.

Still, more silence.

Tonks cleared her throat. “I’ll have another one of those fizzy things, Fred. Albus, you?”

“Why thank you, Dora. They really are quite refreshing.” Albus said, keeping his eyes trained on the two men at the other end of the bar.

“Another gin and tonic for you, Remus? James, again? Oh, Lily, you’ve barely touched yours. Here, have a fresh ale.” Fred bustled about, ignoring the silent parties, and things returned to their jovial state once again.

After a few minutes Severus quietly took his leave of them. Stunned, Sirius barely noticed the flutter of black fabric before it disappeared beyond the door. He sat there, arguing with himself, occasionally muttering something, or fidgeting with his hands. Another minute- or perhaps a year- passed, before Sirius chuckled quietly to himself and left the pub without saying goodbye.

Once the door closed behind the enthusiastic Gryffindor everyone exhaled audibly, relief flooding through them at last.

“Merlin, I thought that’d never happen!” Fred bellowed.

“It’s been a long time coming.” Laughed James before squeezing his wife around the middle to steady himself.

“Love conquers all!” Lily leaned into James’ embrace and sighed. “I never understood how the houses could be so divided.” She mused.

Remus sat quietly, his eyes trained on the tiny bubbles dancing about in the untouched glass before him.

Tonks threaded her fingers through her husbands. “Are you alright?” she asked him, knowing the cause for his reservations.

“Yes.” He replied with a gloomy look behind his eyes.

“You’re not feeling a bit jealous, are you?” Tonks wrapped her arms around Remus’s neck and hugged him tightly.

“Maybe just a little.” Remus wanted to say more, but his thoughts were too disheveled. He was worried that it might not go well for Sirius or Severus. He desperately wanted them to work it out. He needed them to work it out. He cared too much about both of them for this not to work out. “We’ve all led such complicated lives.” Remus stared knowingly into Tonks’ eyes before placing a gentle kiss to her lips. “If anyone deserves to be happy, it’s them.”

Tonks smiled lovingly back at Remus, an impish quirk to her smile. “You know you could join them right now and neither would turn you away.”

Remus chuckled, nervously. “Not sure if we’re there yet, love. Besides, they each need time to figure this whole thing out.”

Tonks gave him that same pitying look that James used on Sirius.

Remus rushed to reassure her. “Next time, perhaps. If all goes well.” he stated, wondering- not for the first time- just how the three of them would manage. Then before he could stop himself, he added anxiously, “It will go well, won’t it? They’re not going to destroy one another in the process?”

“Of course, love. They’re both stubborn as Hell, but they’ll get through this. It’s on the Borad, remember?” Tonks replied mirthfully. The crinkle at the corner of her eyes showed her excitement.

Remus couldn’t help his own delightful smile.

“Alright, everyone!” Fred announced, pulling a small Scoreboard from under the counter. The secret one. The one that neither Sirius nor Severus had ever known about. “Let’s see if anyone had them down for today.” He glanced at the bets and sure enough there was an enormous sum placed on the current date. “Oh, I should have known! Albus, you win again. You’re always right, you know.” Fred scoffed, shaking his head.

“I may have had my fair share of good guesses over the years, Fred, but I would never presume to be right in all things.” Albus corrected.

Fred chuckled. “Fair enough. Well, what do you want to do with your winnings?” His wand was pointed at the secret Scoreboard, ticking off the long-awaited victory.

“As always, please redistribute them back into this establishment so the esteemed patrons can partake of more free drink.” Albus held out his palms in kindness.

“Cheers!” Everyone shouted at the brilliant headmaster.

Albus blushed. “It’s nothing really. I just love happy endings.”
