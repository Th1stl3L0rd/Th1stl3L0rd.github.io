Prologue

All of it.

Or lack thereof.

Because it was gone.

GAH!!!

What a fucking mess.

And unless he was mistaken- Severus knew he wasn’t- this was not a random gathering, but the congregation of an entire Swarm.

The only thing he could think of that might cause this to happen would be if they were agitated into such a state by an external force. Something that no witch or wizard in their right mind would ever dare to…

Realization shot through him so sharply that visible sparks flew from the tips of his fingers.

WEASLEYS!!!

RAGE… NO WORDS… TOO FAR!!!

Then, of course, when he stepped in the dragon dung that Pomona left for him, which must have been held under a stasis charm to preserve it, well…”

“I never would have done such a thing if I’d known he’d suffer like that, Albus! You must believe me!” Sprout intimated, her fly away hair swaying as she shook her head.

“I do believe you, my dear.” Then the Headmaster sighed and shook his head, looking about the room for a moment lost in thought. At last he spoke, though whether it was to his staff or into the aether, no one knew. “Severus has been affected most egregiously by all of this… but the pixies… I am truly at a loss.” Albus threw up his hands halfheartedly, then focused on the anxious face of his Herbology teacher once more. “As you said, Pomona, it isn’t Pixie Season yet.” She shook her head briskly in agreement.

“Well, that’s enough rumination for one day, I think.” Albus said. “Let’s get to work, shall we?” The professors looked at him, confusion and trepidation warring within their features. “George.” He addressed with authority. “You were responsible for casting ‘Flashy Flora’, so I will need you to run along and cast a Finite on Severus’ quarters as well. We will have no more unstable magic loose about his rooms.” Another harsh stare over his half-moon specs. “Oh, and mind how you go. If he catches you, you’re liable to be damaged beyond repair.” he added.

George nodded fervently.

“Fred,” Albus commanded, “you will escort Bathsheda, Filius, and Pomona back with you to your Assignment. They are very eager to assist you in any way that they can after this debacle.” He looked at them severely and they heartily stammered their agreement.

Albus formally concluded the private meeting and dismissed his staff. However, as everyone was preparing to leave, the door flew open and Hagrid burst into the room huffing and puffing. He took a moment to collect himself- though still huffing and puffing terribly- staring confusedly at the Pixie Dust on the floor, then looked back up at everyone before zoning in on the Headmaster. “Oh, good! You’re ‘ere!” He yelled exasperatedly; his breathing marginally less sporadic now. “Oh, Professor Dumbledore… I… oh, i’s jus’ terrible. I don’ righ'ly know where teh begin.”

Albus sighed and held up his hand. “Calm yourself, Rubeus.” He waited a beat for his shaggy gamekeeper to get his breathing fully under control, then added, “Now, explain the situation to me. One thing at a time, if you please.”

“Ah, well.” He cleared his throat. “Yeh see, I was… well. I ‘ad this special treat planned for me third-year students.” He was twisting his sausage-sized fingers in earnest, looking at his feet. “An’ it seems tha’… well… thin’s got a bit out o’ hand.”

Albus looked intently at the bumbling half-giant, using his last patience of the day to remain calm. “Go on.” He coaxed.

“Righ', well… The thin’ is. I ‘ad a Colony o’ pixies stashed away in the Forbidden Forest. Yeh know, somethin’ really grand for the start o’ summer term. I wanted ter teach ‘em how the spot the diff’rences before and after the little thin’s molt for their Swarm. Yeh know, teh keep 'em safe.”

Albus knew this would not end well, but he entreated Hagrid to continue anyway. “You have been keeping pixies in the Forest?”

“Well, yeah. Hatched ‘em and everythin’ all by meself.” He said proudly.

“You hatched an entire Colony of pixies?” Albus couldn’t entirely keep the surprise from his voice.

Fred and George looked on in amazement but without surprise. “Do you have any of the eggs left?” George asked eagerly.

Albus gave them a hard stare. He gestured with his hand for Hagrid to continue.

“Ah,” Hagrid began nervously again, “well, it wouldn’ be a proper lesson withou’ enough creatures ter go round for each student, would it?” He asked confused.

Albus nodded, measuring his response. “Of course not, Rubeus. However, don’t you think it might have been better to choose a less hazardous creature in such large numbers?”

“Oh, i‘s not tha’ many, really. About two ‘undred, is all.”

“Two hundred!” Fred exclaimed.

“Yeah.” Hagrid beamed. “There’s Mupsy an’ Doplin an’ Egar an’ Slimpy,” he began reciting their names, counting on his fingers.

The twins had a time of it hiding their laughter. They kept glancing at Albus, his posture growing more severe by the second, his face showing clearly that he was at a loss for words as well as what to do about this latest development. All the while, Hagrid continued naming each pixie from his precious Colony.

“- Shawny, Mumu, Itsy, oh an’ yeh can’ forget Dippy. No one‘ll forget ‘im in a hurry.” He leaned over to the twins conspiratorially and added. “ee’s a righ' tricky one.” He glanced back at Albus and noted the perplexed stare on his face. Mistaking it for disappointment, he quickly added. “Oh, bu’ then there’s LuLu! She ‘as the most beau’iful scales. She’ll really be somethin’ when she ‘as her firs’ molt.”

“That’s incredible, Hagrid!” George added, trying not to lose it.

“Ah, ‘twas easy enough.” The half-giant blushed.

At that the twins couldn’t help but guffaw, holding their middles and bracing themselves to keep each other upright.

“That’s enough.” Albus directed his impatience at the twins, who immediately fell silent but couldn’t help the involuntary grins that kept spreading across their faces, no matter how hard they tried to tamp them down.

“Now, Rubeus.” Albus began what felt like the one millionth remonstrative speech about his disastrous escapades in husbandry. “I appreciate that you felt the need to educate our students so thoroughly on the dangers of Pixie Swarms, but I must ask you not to choose such a stressful category for the third-year classes again.”

“Bu’ they’re jus’ pixies. Totally ‘armless. Tha’s why I chose ‘em. Didn’ want nothin’ too dangerous for such youngins.”

Albus released a long sigh. “I will agree that they are usually nothing more than an irritant in small numbers, but a Swarm can be unpredictable. It is something that should be taught in theory only, until they are preparing for O.W.L.s, and would be even better suited for N.E.W.T.s.” He gave Hagrid a severe look to drive his point home.

“Oh. Righ'.” Hagrid looked down bashfully.

Albus sighed again, already guessing the answer to the question he was about to pose. “You said there was a problem with this Colony of yours?”

“Ah, yeah. Well…” He began twisting his fingers again. “I was entertainin’ Remus this afternoon when I noticed ‘em…” He paused, trying to find the best way of telling Albus what had happened. “I looked ou’ the window and saw ‘em takin’ off from the Forest. Professor Dumbledore… they aren’t teh start their Swarmin’ ‘til the kids are back from their break.” He hung his head low shaking it from side to side. ‘I ‘ad it all planned ou’. I don’ understand what ‘appened…”

Albus remained calm, dutifully assessing the situation. “Where is the Swarm now?”

“Oh, they flew off t’wards the castle. Tha’s why I lef’ Remus in such a hurry… an’ I tried ter keep up with ‘em, bu’ they’re righ' fast.” Hagrid spoke intently. “I followed their trail o’ Dust, though. Though’ for sure they’d go straight ter the greenhouses, since tha’d be the only place teh find fresh dragon dung and they need it for their nests yeh know…” He trailed off, his brow furrowed in concentration. “Bu’ they shouldn’ve been able ter smell it from so deep in the Forest. I made sure teh keep ‘em far enough away, so they wouldn’ go off before it was time.”

Albus stared at Hagrid curiously. “Regardless of whether or not there was fresh dragon dung available, the pixies should not have Swarmed. Their mating season does not begin for another two months.”

“Ah, well… as to tha’… You’re righ' o’ course.” Hagrid cleared his throat nervously. “Bu’ I, well… I migh’ve ‘elped ‘em along a bit.” He looked at the floor.

“What do you mean by ‘helped’?” Albus once again guessed the answer and braced himself for the words that would soon follow.

“Yeh know, jus’ a wee bit o’ magic ter speed up the process. I needed ‘em ready before summer, so’s the kids could get a chance ter see ‘em. Then they’d know how teh spot ‘em if the pixies were Swarmin’, and they could stay clear of ‘em. Like I said. An’ then once they’d learned tha’ bit, I was goin’ teh show the kids how to tame ‘em.” Hagrid said with conviction; his explanation completely logical to him. He rushed ahead with his words, the urge to make them understand overwhelming. “Now, I know there’s people ou’ there as hates ‘em and thin's their righ' nasty, bu’ it ain' true I tell yeh. I remember ‘ow bad it was when Lockhart was ‘ere, turnin’ all the kids against ‘em. He shouldn’ve done tha’. As I said, their ‘armless and make great pets, ‘slong as yeh know what yer’re doin’.”

The room was silent. As bizarre as Hagrid’s previous ventures into creature husbandry were, this one was definitely at the top of the list.

Albus turned towards the hearth once more in thought. It was several moments before he nodded and hummed. Without turning he addressed Hagrid. “So, what you are telling me, Rubeus, is that you raised an entire Colony of pixies… Treated them with magic so that they would enter their Swarming state earlier than usual… And that they have escaped into the Grounds in search of dung for their nests so that they may beget the next generation?”

“Yeah, tha’d be abou’ righ', sir.” Hagrid confirmed, furrowing his brow. Then his eyes grew round as saucers as realization set in. “Oh, no.”

Albus turned around to face his gamekeeper. “If you hurry, you may be able to stop their molting before it’s too late. If you are unsuccessful, please inform me immediately, so that I might gather the needed help to deal with the situation. The last thing we need is for a Colony to take over Hogwarts.”

“Yes, sir!” Hagrid nodded vigorously and dashed for the door, nearly ripping it from the hinges as he shut it behind him.

Albus sighed yet again. “At least now we know why Severus was attacked.”

Pomona made another attempt to apologize, but Albus held up his hand, then his eyes closed in thought. The staff stared at him, waiting for his decision.

“Well, you all have your Assignments. Off with you, now.” The aged headmaster watched them leave one by one through the Floo. He shook his head, exiting through the door and taking the stairs to his office. The long walk would do him good.

Albus took his time climbing each step, thinking over all the possible ways in which he could salvage his plans by the end of summer term. When he reached his office, he went straight to his grand claw-foot desk, passing Fawkes on the way and stroking his feathers in the process. He sat in his ornate highbacked chair and sighed before spelling open the locked drawer at the bottom right-hand side. He produced a seldom-used, ornate bottle of amber liquid and its matching crystal. He was going to need a very large brandy indeed.

Break

The remaining week before Easter break flew by and Severus could not have been more grateful. He had spent every spare moment- save those reserved for his communal meal- shut up in his lab, painstakingly brewing everything he could think of to rid himself of the lingering sparkle on his skin and in his hair, to no avail.

In those moments when he’d been forced to take his breakfasts with the rest of the school, he’d caught glimpses of Hagrid and Fang through the windows of the Great Hall speeding about the Grounds, chasing a nasty looking Swarm of pixies- an enormous blue blur, pursued by a rather large brown blur, and followed closely by a much smaller barking grey blur.

The students found this to be hilarious and gave him plenty of excuses to take House points for their merriment. And speaking of merriment… Not one word had been spoken within earshot of him about his appearance the previous Sunday. Not since the initial comment by a group of fourth year students on Monday, anyway. Oh, how the colour drained from their faces when they’d been caught. Severus managed to take more than one hundred points from them in total that morning.

It was pleasing to know that he still commanded such authority after what they’d witnessed. More pleasing still was that Albus believed he’d found a solution to his Pixie Dust problem, although it wasn’t ready for testing yet. In the meantime, the old man made sure to recount all the details pertaining to Serverus’ affliction- which, being an affluent wordsmith with apparent oodles of time on his hands, took ages. Then Albus spent less than ten seconds relating to Severus that the Twins were in fact not responsible for said problem, and spent even less time explaining that he would not reveal the guilty party to him until Severus learned how to ‘calm down’. But the Headmaster assured him, that those responsible for turning him into a sparkling, blue spectacle had been thoroughly punished.

It was highly irritating to be kept in the dark with regards to the perpetrators, but Severus brushed it aside to focus on the other, more important information Albus divulged. Kingsley had come through for him, and not only would there be a meeting during the holiday break, but he’d secured a time to appear before the Wizengamot. There would be a preliminary hearing to reevaluate his sentence! It was the best Wednesday afternoon that Severus had experienced in a very long time.

The last day of spring term arrived and those students who would be leaving on the train back to London were milling about as usual. Severus avoided them where he could but was forced to play sentinel by the Entrance Hall as they wandered out to the Grounds in a shrieking, frivolous mass. When the final dunderhead had vanished down the road- signaling the end of his duties- Severus retreated towards the Dungeons in the hopes that his next attempt to remove the glamour-concealed Dust would be his last. Each experiment had left him more and more drained, until he felt it might not be possible to go on, but he knew he was close to a solution. He just had to be!

Severus was pulled from his musings by Albus, who had caught him just as he was about to enter his quarters.

“Ah, there you are, my boy.”

Severus held his tongue. He was nearly free for two whole weeks to hide himself away. He just needed to appease the old man for a few minutes longer. “Headmaster.” He nodded politely.

“I was just about to make my rounds of the school to ensure that everyone who needs to leave is on their way. Will you join me for a stroll?”

“Actually, I am rather busy at the mo-”

“Splendid.” Albus placed his arm around Severus’ shoulder and steered him towards the steps leading back up to the Ground Floor. He was shuffled along as the Headmaster surveyed the Great Hall, the Entrance Hall, the Staffroom and the various classrooms, alcoves, and antechambers. The only inhabitants they met were Vector- being cornered by Trelawney with a cup of ominous tea leaves- and Filch coming out of his office with Mrs Norris in tow, who decided that now would be the perfect time to discuss his latest strategies for discipling students. Albus made his excuses though, promising Argus a discussion later, and steering Severus towards the staircase.

They ascended to the First Floor and Albus once again checked his surroundings. The hallways and rooms were cleared of all staff and students with the exception of Minerva in her office. Albus waffled on for a bit before she wished them both well and left through the Floo to visit her brothers for the holiday. Then the smiling old loon dragged Severus back out into the hallway again and towards the landing.

“How are you feeling today?” Albus asked politely as they waited for the moving staircase.

“Fine.”

“Have you been successful in removing any more of the Dust?”

Severus forced himself to relax his jaw. “I believe I’m close to a solution.”

“I’m so pleased to hear it, my boy. Oh, hello Poppy!” Pomfrey smiled as she approached from the direction of the Hospital wing. The staircase had settled in front of them awaiting their flight, but Albus chose to make further small talk instead. Severus watched the stairs move off once more and sighed agitatedly.

“Hello, Headmaster. Severus.” The kindly matron offered. She had a traveling bag clutched under one arm.

“Are you on your way out for the holiday, then?” Albus asked jovially.

“Yes. My sister is expecting me for dinner. I do feel terrible for skipping out-”

“Nonsense, my dear! You deserve your time away. Margo will take very good care of things until you return.”

“I know she means well, but she is just out of training at St Mungo’s. Are you sure that-”

“Yes, yes. Not to worry. I’ll be here just in case she needs any help.” Albus was far too cheerful about it.

“Well-”

“Go on, Poppy. We’ll see you in two weeks.” He waved goodbye, and she continued on her way down to the Ground Floor.

The staircase returned, thank merlin, and they began to climb once more. Severus felt his jaw clenching further with each step they took. Just a little longer. You can do this. Let the doddering old fool traipse you all over the castle. You’ll be free soon enough.

“You know,” Albus said suddenly, “the meeting with Kingsley is more for show than anything else. All that bureaucratic balderdash…” He waved his hand about as if shooing a fly. “We both know the Wizengamot will pardon you, they have too much evidence in your favour. They know this as well, always have, ever since Harry testified on the first day of your trial.” Albus continued speaking as if he were discussing the weather. “It was simply a matter of exhausting their efforts to detain you. Now, they no longer have any excuse to prolong your imprisonment. You are as good as freed already, my boy.”

The old man smiled kindly at him, and Severus could only blink in astonishment that he’d once again been kept in the dark over such a personal matter. Why, oh, why did Albus always have to play his hand so kaleidoscopically?!

“Of course, there is still a mountain of paperwork to be filed…” Albus trailed off, shaking his head at the Ministry’s blatant delaying tactics. “That’s why Kingsley was certain you would be kept here until summer break, and possibly through the autumn term. But you will be free. And no later than Christmas. That, I will personally guarantee you.” The Headmaster gazed intently over his half-moon specs at the man he would call son if given the chance.

And Severus believed him. For the first time in his life, he had no doubt that Albus would keep his word. It was strange. There had always been lingering doubt. Ever since he’d lost Lily so many years ago. But now…

Albus went on speaking as if they were sharing gossip over afternoon tea. “Well, that only gives you… roughly seven months to settle all your affairs, does it not?” The Headmaster chuckled. “Yes, I know you’re anxious to get out of Britain as soon as possible, and I don’t blame you after what you’ve been though, my boy.”

Severus could only stare at him as they continued making the rounds.

“I’ve often thought of traveling the world, myself.” Albus continued, oblivious to his perplexed employee. “There are so many exciting places to visit! But I suppose there’s not enough time in the year for the Headmaster of Hogwarts and Chief Warlock to go trapsing about the globe, is there?”

Once again, Severus was stunned by the bizarre admission. How was he to respond to such a thing? Surely, he couldn’t reveal his own desires to run off to-

“I guess if there was only one place I could ever go,” Albus resumed, “it would be the South Pacific. Fiji, to be exact. Did you know they have a lovely little magical village down there? Yes, it’s quite beautiful from what I’ve been told.”

Severus just barely suppressed a gasp, his footsteps nearly faltering. It was only his many years as a spy that kept him from giving anything away. How did he… No. That wasn’t possible. Albus couldn’t know about that. It was his most precious secret. He’d never told anyone, not even Dream Lily. Surely, this was just a coincidence. But Severus knew the wise old man far too well for that. How does he always know everything…

And Albus continued talking, even with the now stiff-gated Potions Master eyeing him suspiciously. “-iguanas, and bats. Many birds nest there as well, you know. And, of course, there’s mangos everywhere, and they have those lovely little rambutans, and dragon fruit.”

This was simply too much for Severus. What was the old codger playing at with all that stuff about Fiji? Was he deliberately trying to taunt him with it as a means of exacting a final measure of torture against him? Severus felt as if he were slowly being driven insane by want of such vivid descriptions.

Albus just kept on going, “Oh, and there’s this chap- from Liverpool, I believe- living there now. He’s rather eccentric from what I understand. Has a few sheep and some cows, and he breeds horses, apparently. I’m sure it looks a strange sight, but there you have it.” He chuckled fondly, then went on rambling. “Of course, one needn’t travel so far away. Not if one feels so inclined to take a deeper look around them and notice just how unfathomable life is right outside their very own door. You could spend centuries looking and never see it all…”

The Headmaster sighed happily. “Perhaps just a little holiday wouldn’t hurt though, a small distraction from the mundane… Yes, I think that’s what I’d do, if I had the time. Then, oh, wouldn’t it be a grand thing indeed to settle down in a little cottage, tending my marigolds and whatnot… Giving me the time needed to catch up on things long neglected so very close to home.”

That was it. Severus couldn’t take the old goat’s mumbled nonsense any longer. He nearly had a string of vitriol out of his mouth when Albus placed a hand upon his arm to slow him down.

“Oh, there’s Argus again. He’ll be wanting to set up that meeting.” Filch was panting up the corridor towards them. “Hello, Argus.” Albus put up his hand to stop the caretaker in his tracks. “No, I haven’t the time to talk of punishments just now. Run along with the others and enjoy your holiday. And for Merlin’s sake, try to relax while you’re gone. I hate seeing you in such a strop when you return.” Filch stared incredulously back at him for a moment before wandering off, his mangy old cat following at his heels.

“That man” Albus said, “is far too grumpy. It wouldn’t hurt him to smile or even laugh once in a while… Perhaps if I started giving him the odd job to do around the Grounds with Hagrid, it would lighten his mood. All that lovely sunshine is bound to be good for him.”

Severus cringed at the thought, his mind rapidly filling with the rather scary image of a ‘happy’ Filch frolicking along with Hagrid, while Fang and Ms Norris trotted merrily behind them.

“Well, now. Everything seems to be going along splendidly.” Albus chimed as he finished surveying the Third Floor.

Why does he always get so giddy over the holidays? Merlin, it’s even worse this year- he’s practically bouncing along the corridor! “Indeed.” Severus answered curtly.

Albus ignored the Potions Master’s disdain and continued to hum jauntily as they ascended the remaining floors, where the older man took great care in assessing the vacancy of every nook and cranny. When they’d finally made it to the Seventh Floor, Albus did the same, then made an additional- albeit hasty- check that revealed no lingering students other than those electing to stay behind. He greeted them with such mirth that the students couldn’t help but return his infectious smile.

Severus willed his jaw not to crack under the immense pressure building there. He was just about to tell the old man off when Albus steered him along another corridor. That was odd. Severus felt sure they would be adjourning to the Headmaster’s office now that the rounds had been complete.

“Ah, here we are at last!” Albus spread his arm out towards a bare patch of wall, beaming.

Severus took a moment to realize exactly where they were. He had expected to see the charred remains of an entryway and splintered door. But there was only the ancient stone before him, and it was no longer secured against intrusion by heavy wards. “Albus, this is the former entrance to the Room of Requirement.”

“Ah, I’m so glad you noticed, my boy! Yes, and the door is missing, isn’t it?”

“You appear to be correct, Headmaster.”

“Yes. Well, why do you think it is missing?”

“You have finally renovated this area of the castle?” He deadpanned, inspecting the bare wall closely for emphasis. It was the only place they hadn’t been able to heal.

“Yes… and repaired it to its original state.”

Severus whipped his head around quickly. “Repaired?”

“Yes. The Room of Requirement is sentient once more and-”

“How is that possible?!” Severus had seen the damage; he knew it was irreparable. Nothing could withstand Fiendfyre!

“Ah, my boy. There are many secrets within Hogwarts.”

Severus just stared at him; brow furrowed.

“In short, it was a lot of hard work performed by your fellow staff members. They were all quite enthusiastic, I must say.” Albus beamed.

“What?” Severus was momentarily stunned, then incensed by the man’s gall. “Why would you even bother with such a thing? Surely, you realize how dangerous that place can be?!”

“Yes, but I have ensured that this time, the room will obey and appear or disappear for the Headmaster, as long as that person is of pure intention and without malice of any kind in their heart.” The twinkle was overwhelming in his eye. “There is no more reason to fret. There will always be a safeguard in place from this day forward.”

Severus was perplexed. “But… what rational purpose does it serve? Why… what-” Severus broke off abruptly, incapable of understanding why the old loon would waste his time on something so frivolous. Then he remembered that this was the same man who made everyone personalized Christmas crackers.

“Surely, you remember how the Room works?” Albus asked. He waited a beat, letting the younger man gain his bearings before he continued. “It gives you whatever you need, Severus.”

“And…”

“And what you need will be found through that door.” Albus inclined his head towards the pristine woodgrain that had appeared before them.

Severus contemplated the aged man’s words. He would find what he needed…

The cure to rid him of this abominable Pixie Dust!

A strange rush of joy, relief, and trepidation swept across his body, and Severus thought for one moment he might sway and possibly collapse from the force of it. The Dust would be gone for good. No more exhaustive cloaking and glamours. But… it wasn’t possible… was it? No. It couldn’t be. But it must be so. Albus wouldn’t be so cruel as to joke about a thing like that. He looked up hopeful at the Headmaster. “Must I walk past it three times, or will it allow me entrance now?”

“Normally, it would require the traditional three passes, and the intent, but as I am the Headmaster of Hogwarts, it will obey me and allow you entrance.” Albus said fondly while the younger man simply stared at him. “Go on, then.”

Severus still couldn’t believe his luck, but he tamped down his misgivings, unwilling to let this opportunity pass him by. He reached for the doorknob and began to turn it, his hand betraying a slight tremor as he did so while another thrill of trepidation sped through him.

“Oh, and Severus?” Albus called out to him.

Severus glanced over his shoulder.

“Good luck and enjoy your holiday.” His smile was broad, and his twinkling eyes shone more brightly than ever.

“Thank you.” Severus barely managed. The sheer happiness emanating from Albus threatened to overwhelm him entirely.

He turned back to the door, focusing his attention fully on turning the knob. There was a faint click and then the door was pushing inward, revealing a rather cozy looking room. Severus stepped across the threshold, confused. It looked more like someone’s personal quarters than a lab.

How am I to brew the potion needed to treat these insufferable sparkles with this?!

Severus shut the door silently behind him as he looked about the room. Perhaps it housed something else meant to cure him? The wall to his right held a row of old bookcases with an assortment of tomes, some new and pristine, some ancient and weathered, others only mildly dog-eared. A small glass-fronted cabinet full of what appeared to be various forms of alcohol was to his left near the door, a large bottle of Ogden’s stationed prominently on top. Further along the wall was a grand four-poster bed with- ugh- crimson curtains. A large hearth was before him, a merry fire crackling away, with a deep pile cream-coloured rug set before it. Surrounding this were several chairs made of soft black leather and a large comfortable-looking sofa full of-

“LUPIN!”

Remus glanced over his shoulder at Severus from his position, dislodging someone from their respite against his arm. A head full of luxurious, curly black hair shifted upward to reveal-

“BLACK!”

“Hello, Severus.” Remus said, his brow furrowed slightly. “What are you doing here?”

“You what?” Severus found it very difficult to pull his thoughts together. This was the absolute last thing he’d expected.

“It’s not that I’m not pleased to see you, but we weren’t expecting you.” Remus replied kindly, standing from the sofa.

Sirius jumped up quickly to stand beside his lover. “Yeah. We’re on assignment from Dumbledore, to check out the new Room and make sure it’s in working order. He never said anything about you being here.” He quipped irritably.

Severus drew his wand on the pair before him, panic gnawing at the back his mind as he surmised the three of them were alone together.

“Whoa, Severus.” Remus cautioned; his hands raised before him. “What’s the problem?”

What’s the problem? “YOU are the problem! BOTH of you!” He flicked his wand towards Black, who had drawn his own wand looking even more perturbed than before.

“You intruded upon us, you Great Bat!” Sirius shouted.

“Now, Sirius-”

“No, Remus! Not this time. He comes in here, in the middle of our assignment and thinks he can just badger us about. Well, I’ll not stand for it!”

Remus sighed and rubbed his forehead. “Look. I don’t know what’s going on here, but I’m sure Severus didn’t mean any harm to either of us.”

Sirius looked at him incredulously. “Why do you always do that?! You know he’s not worth it!”

“You seemed to think differently last week, and seven months ago, and roughly a year-and-a-half ago as well.” Remus crossed his arms and stared down his husband pointedly.

Sirius pressed his palms into his eyes and groaned.

“Deny it all you want. I remember just how wanton you were on those occasions.”

Sirius sniffed, flicking his hair over his shoulder and not looking at Remus.

Severus viewed their exchange, rapidly piecing together the information and coming to the same conclusion he’d begun panicking over previously. Slowly, and after several deep breaths to calm himself, he said “Dumbledore sent you here?”

“Yes.” Remus supplied, then blinked as recognition stole over his face. “He sent you to check out the room as well?”

“I was invited here to solve my little problem.” Severus ground out, his jaw hurting from the effort of speaking through his tightly clenched teeth.

“The ‘sparkles’? There still affecting you- oh, you’re using a glamour.” Remus nodded in understanding.

Rotuman Cultural References: I chose to have our lovely Thruple be welcomed in the traditional Rotuman manner when they visited the fictional wizard village of Tan hae Hefhefu. The ceremony, called a mamasa, occurs when the person/people who left the island (forau) returns from abroad or deep-sea fishing, but is also used to welcome visitors as well. The word mamasa refers to ‘the act of drying a person that has been wet’ by the salty sea spray. Part of the ceremony involves sitting on a special set of mats (päega), being adorned by a garland of leaves and flowers (tefui), and feasting on foods baked in an oven dug into the ground (koua).
