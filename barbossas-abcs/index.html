Eat a whole bushel of apples

Apple

/ăp′əl/

  • A round, edible fruit produced by an apple tree, which are cultivated worldwide. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found.There are more than 7,500 cultivars of apples. Different cultivars are bred for various tastes and uses, including cooking, eating raw, and cider or apple juice production.

Eat a whole crock of baked beans

Baked Beans

/beɪkt biːnz/

  • Beans that have been soaked and boiled, and then baked with tomato sauce, sugar, salt pork and seasoning. Canned baked beans are not baked, but cooked through steaming. Baked beans originated in Native American cuisine, and are made from beans indigenous to the Americas.

Eat a whole bunch of carrots

Carrot

/kăr′ət/

  • An orange root vegetable, though heirloom variants including purple, black, red, white, and yellow cultivars exist, all of which are domesticated forms of the wild carrot, native to Europe and Southwestern Asia. The plant probably originated in Iran and was cultivated for its leaves and seeds.

Eat a whole plate of donuts

Donut

/dō′nŭt″, -nət/

  • A deep-fried piece of dough or batter, commonly made in a toroidal or ellipsoidal shape, and mixed with various sweeteners and flavors, sometimes filled with jelly, custard or cream. Doughnut is the traditional spelling, while donut is the simplified version; the terms are used interchangeably.

Eat a whole basket of eggs

Egg

/eg/

  • The hard-shelled reproductive body produced by a bird and especially by the common domestic chicken. Most eggs laid by domestic fowl, except those specifically set aside for hatching, are not fertilized but are sold mainly for human consumption. Eggs produced in quantity come from chickens, ducks, geese, turkeys, guinea fowl, pigeons, pheasants, and quail.

Eat a whole pot of fondue

Fondue

/fɒnˈdjuː/

  • Fondue is a Swiss dish of melted cheese and wine served in a communal pot over a portable stove heated with a candle or spirit lamp, and eaten by dipping bread and sometimes vegetables or other foods into the cheese using long-stemmed forks. Dessert fondues are made with either chocolate, coconut, honey, caramel, or marshmallow.

Golden Syrup

/ɡəʊl.dən sɪr.əp/

  • Golden syrup or light treacle is a thick, amber-coloured form of inverted sugar syrup made by the process of refining sugar cane or sugar beet juice into sugar. It is used in a variety of baking recipes and desserts. It has an appearance and consistency similar to honey.

Horse- radish

Eat a whole gallon of ice cream

Ice Cream

/ˌaɪs ˈkriːm/

  • A frozen dessert made from milk or cream (or non-dairy alternatives), sweetened, and often flavored with additives such as vanilla, cocoa, and/or fruit. Eat a whole pan of jambalaya

Jamba- laya

/ˌdʒæm.bəˈleɪ.ə/

  • Jambalaya is a savory rice dish that developed in the U.S. state of Louisiana fusing together African, Spanish, and French influences, consisting mainly of meat (ham, sausage, chicken) and/or seafood (shrimp or oysters), and vegetables mixed with rice and spices.

Eat a whole bottle of ketchup

Ketchup

/ˈketʃ.ʌp/

  • Ketchup or catsup is a table condiment made from tomatoes, sugar, vinegar, seasonings and spices (onions, allspice, coriander, cloves, cumin, garlic, mustard and sometimes include celery, cinnamon, or ginger). Early recipes for ketchup contained mushrooms, oysters, mussels, egg whites, grapes, or walnuts, among other ingredients.

Eat a whole head of lettuce

Lettuce

/ˈlet.ɪs/

  • An annual plant of the family Asteraceae mostly grown as a leaf vegetable. The leaves are most often used raw in green salads, although lettuce is also seen in other kinds of food, such as sandwiches, wraps and soups; it can also be grilled. It was originally farmed by the ancient Egyptians for seed oil.

Eat a whole jar of mayonnaise

Mayo- nnaise

/ˌmeɪ.əˈneɪz/

  • Mayonnaise, or "mayo", is a thick, creamy sauce with a rich and tangy taste that is commonly used on sandwiches, salads, and more. It also forms the base of various other sauces, such as tartar sauce, and rémoulade. Mayo is an emulsion of oil, egg yolk, and either vinegar or lemon juice.

Eat a whole tray of nougat

Nougat

/ˈnuː.ɡɑː, -ɡət/

  • Nougat is a family of confections made with sugar or honey, roasted nuts, whipped egg whites, and sometimes chopped candied fruit. The consistency of nougat is chewy, and it is used in a variety of candy bars and chocolates. The word nougat comes from Occitan nogat, which means 'nutted' or 'nutty'.

Eat a whole bowl of olives

Olive

/ˈɑː.lɪv, ˈɒl.ɪv/

  • A species of small evergreen in the family Oleaceae, which includes species such as lilac, jasmine, and forsythia. The fruit are small and either black or green. It was first cultivated on the Greek island of Crete about 3500 BC and was used as fuel, fertilizer, ointment, lubricant, medical remedy, and food.

Eat a whole jar of pickles

Pickle

/ˈpɪk.əl/

  • A vegetable or fruit in vinegar or in salty water, especially a cucumber. The fermentation process is executed either by immersing the cucumbers in an acidic solution or through souring by lacto-fermentation. Pickles are referred to as gherkins in Britain, Ireland, South Africa, and New Zealand.

Quiche

/kiːʃ/

Eat a whole red velvet cake

Red Velvet Cake

/ˌred ˈvel.vɪt ˌkeɪk/

  • A red-colored layer cake with cream cheese or ermine icing. Origin unknown. It is popular in the Southern U.S. and has been served as a dessert at New York City's Waldorf-Astoria hotel since the 1920s. It is eaten on Juneteenth as a symbolic food that represents the bloodshed and sacrifice of enslaved African Americans.

Eat a whole log of salami

Salami

/səˈlɑː.mi/

  • Salami is a fermented and air-dried meat, typically made from pork, but may also be made from beef, veal, venison, turkey or even goose. Historically, it was popular among European peasants because it can be stored at room temperature for up to 45 days once cut, supplementing a potentially meager or inconsistent supply of fresh meat.

Eat a whole roast turkey

Turkey

/ˈtɝː.ki, ˈtɜː.ki/

  • The turkey is a large bird native to North America. The earliest turkeys evolved over 20 million years ago. They share a recent common ancestor with grouse, pheasants, and other fowl. The wild turkey species is the ancestor of the domestic turkey, which was domesticated approximately 2,000 years ago by indigenous peoples.

Eat a whole bowl of umeboshi

Umeboshi

/ˌuː.məˈbɒʃ.i/

  • Umeboshi are pickled ume fruits common in Japan. They are often referred to as "plums" but are more closely related to apricots. They are extremely sour and salty. They are usually served as a side dish for rice or eaten on rice balls for breakfast and lunch. They are occasionally served boiled or seasoned for dinner.

Eat a whole jar of Vegemite

Vegemite

/ˈvedʒ.i.maɪt/

  • Vegemite is a thick, dark brown, strong flavored Australian food spread for toast, sandwiches, crumpets, cracker biscuits and more. It is made from leftover brewers' yeast extract with various vegetable and spice additives. It is salty, slightly bitter, malty and has an umami flavour similar to beef bouillon.

Eat a whole plate of wasabi

Wasabi

/wəˈsɑː.bi/

  • A plant of the family Brassicaceae, which also includes horseradish and mustard. Native to Japan, the Russian Far East, and the Korean Peninsula. It grows naturally along stream beds in mountain river valleys in Japan. Wasabi is grown for its rhizomes, which are ground into a paste as a pungent condiment for sushi and other foods.

XO sauce

/eks əʊ sɑːs/

  • XO sauce is a spicy seafood sauce made of roughly-chopped dried seafood, including dried scallops, fish and shrimp, which are cooked with chilli peppers, onions and garlic. It originated in Hong Kong and is commonly used in southern Chinese regions such as Guangdong.

Eat a whole tub of yogurt

Yogurt

/ˈjoʊ.ɡɚt, ˈjɒɡ.ət/

  • A food produced by bacterial fermentation of milk. It has a creamy texture and a tart flavor profile. Cow's milk is most common, but milk from water buffalo, goats, ewes, mares, camels, and yaks can also be used to produce yogurt. Due to the natural sourness, many people prefer to offset it by adding fruit or other sweeteners.
