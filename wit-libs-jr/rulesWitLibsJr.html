How to use your Silly Words
Common Nouns:

A Common Noun is a word that names something, such as a Person, Place, Thing, or Idea.

Examples:

Person: old woman, baby, mailman, cousin, teacher, sister, pilot, family, team

Place: pond, courtyard, bedroom, sky, island, bathroom, cupboard, ocean, canyon

Thing: oatmeal, cat, tractor, lollipop, chalkboard, fish, toenail, gumball, spoon

Idea: life, courage, advice, power, freedom, laughter, joy

Proper Nouns:

A Proper Noun is the specific name of a Person, Place, or Thing and is ALWAYS capitalized.

Examples:

Person: Alex, Mario, Mr. Rogers, Big Bird, Ms. Mikenna, Cookie Monster

Place: Sierra Leone, Mount Everest, Mariana Trench, Holland Park

Thing: International Space Station, Nintendo Switch, Playdoh, Crayola

Plural Nouns:

A Plural Noun refers to more than one Person, Place, Thing, or Idea. MOST Plural Nouns end in -s or -es.

Examples:

Person: women, babies, teachers, doctors, firemen, bakers, children

Place: streets, fields, houses, caves, ceilings, mountains, states, outer space

Thing: cats, armchairs, fish, sheep, hammers, bushes, mangoes, knives

Verbs:

A Verb is an action word that tells a noun what to do. There are many types of verbs.

Examples:

Dynamic (active) Verbs: eat, walk, learn, grow, sleep, play, talk, write, run, read, become, go, be, am, are, is, would, could, shall.

Stative (state of being) Verbs: love, hate, like, prefer, want, doubt, seem, know, own, understand.

Present Participle Verbs (ing): jumping, flying, falling, laughing, eating, clapping, smiling, skipping, playing.

Past-tense Verbs: jumped, blew, fell, shot, ate, left, hid, laughed, sheltered, weathered, belted, crowned.

Adjectives:

Adjectives modify (or describe) the qualities or state of being of nouns. There are three degrees of adjectives.

Examples:

Absolute: cool, stinky, eager, green, happy, determined, brave, prickly, tall, imaginative, shy, lovely.

Comparative: cooler, messier, ickier, braver, more determined, more rigid, more awful.

Superlative: coolest, stinkiest, most mischievous, most daring.

Adverbs:

Adverbs modify (or describe) verbs or other adverbs to answer the questions how, where, when, to what extent, and why.

Examples: very, quickly, bravely, messily, luckily, blindly, hauntingly, vaguely, supremely, yesterday, here, there, now, too.
